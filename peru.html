PERU

Lake Titicaca, pictured above, is the world's highest navigable lake. It sits over two and a half miles above sea level and is 922 feet at its deepest point.


The ancient Incan Empire settled in modern day Peru. They built many cities and buildings, including the Machu Piccu.

The Nazca Lines, pictured above are located in Peru - they are believed to be ancient drawings only visible from the air.


These figures have perplexed people for centuries because they require aircraft to be viewed and appreciated. It is unclear how ancient humans were able to create these lines without aircraft.
